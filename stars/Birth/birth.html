birth

Stars are born in vast, cold clouds of gas and dust known as molecular clouds. These clouds can be massive, containing up to millions of times the mass of the Sun and spanning large distances. Inside these clouds, the cold conditions cause gas to clump together, forming dense regions. These regions grow in mass either by collecting more material or colliding with other clumps, increasing their gravitational pull. Eventually, the gravitational forces cause these dense clumps to collapse.
